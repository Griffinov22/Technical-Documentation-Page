Introduction

I am about to blow your mind. Your favorite pick me up beverage you drink in the morning comes from a seed. Coffee comes from the a species of Coffea plant. The coffea plant is a tree that can be a whopping 30 feet tall! Each tree grows cherries that contain a seed that, you guessed it, gets turned into coffee by roasting it. Coffee is believed to have came from Yemen, but many disagree. If you would like to delve deeper and form your own oppinion, visit this page that goes more in depth on that topic.

Roast level

The coffee tree produces cherries that are soon to be pulped, or taken the outer layer of the cherry away, leaving behind a green oval-shaped bean. That been is then roasted and shipped out to consumers who make the drink we love. Coffee roasters roast the bean for different lengths to produce a different roast levels. The most common roast levels in order from lightest roast to darkest roast is:

  • Light Roast
    • Light City
    • Half City
    • Cinnamon Roast
    • New England Roast
  • Medium Roast
    • Regular Roast
    • American Roast
    • City Roast
    • Breakfast Roast
  • Medium-Dark Roast
    • Full-City Roast
    • After Dinner Roast
    • Vienna Roast
  • Dark Roast
    • French Roast
    • Italian Roast
    • Espresso Roast
    • Continental Roast
    • New Orleans Roast
    • Spanish Roast

This list is not supposed to intimidate you about roast-level. Simply, each roast is put into a section of light, medium, medium-dark, and dark roast. Darker roasts tend to have a darker almost black color along with being the most oily of the beans because the heat of the roaster brings all the moisture out of the bean. Lighter roasts, on the other hand, tend to be light brown and heavier because less moisture has been roasted out of the bean.
